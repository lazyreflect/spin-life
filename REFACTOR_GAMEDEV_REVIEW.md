# Game-Dev Review of REFACTOR.md — Future-Development Suggestions

> Produced by a workflow of 7 character-roll game-dev personas (rarity, live-ops/content,
> procedural narrative, balance/telemetry, determinism/shareability, moddability,
> player-meta) reviewing [REFACTOR.md](REFACTOR.md), consolidated by a lead designer.

## 1. TL;DR

The refactor is the right *engineering* move, sequenced well, and the team backs landing it largely as written — but to a designer it is a "no behavior change" internal-quality pass that risks shipping a beautifully-maintainable model wrapped around the exact same flat, single-session game it is today. Three capabilities the doc never names are the actual unlock: a **seeded, injectable RNG** (every persona who touched determinism converged here — it is a behavior-preserving plumbing step that belongs *before* §8 step 1, and it makes the entire §7 safety net real), a **collection/rarity-tier meta layer** (the doc fixes the *math* of rarity in step 7 but never the *legibility* — and `prestige` is already authored and rendered nowhere), and **designer/author tooling** (schemas, a balance diff dashboard, runtime-tunable drop rates) that turns the new catalog from a destination into a pipeline. The single biggest miss is that the doc treats rarity and narrative — *the product of a roll game* — as plumbing: cleaning the formula without redesigning what rarity *means*, how it *reads*, and whether two events on a card form a *story*. Land the 9 steps, but fold in the seed primitive (step 0.5), promote validation to versioned schemas (step 1), and design the events/copy schema for a narrative director *before* step 5 freezes it — those three insertions are cheap now and catastrophic to retrofit later.

## 2. Consensus themes

**Theme A — Seeded, injectable RNG is the missing foundational primitive.**
Backed by: Rarity, Balance/Telemetry, Determinism/Shareability, Player-Meta (4 of 7, independently).
Why it matters: The doc's entire safety story (§7 Prereq-0, the "stats unchanged within tol" gates on every §8 row) is unenforceable while the model draws from the global RNG scattered across `stats.js`/`roll.js`/`events.js` plus `randn()`'s module-global `_spare` carry. Without seeds the sim can only assert *distributional tolerance* — draw-order regressions and per-life breakage hide under the tolerance band, and you cannot bisect a balance bug. It is also the substrate for *every* meta feature the personas want (shareable permalinks, daily seeds, golden-replay tests, leaderboards). Critically, all four note it gets strictly *harder* to retrofit each step you defer it: once `events.json`/`copy.json` (step 5) and the shape/fn registry exist, every pool-pick is one more uncontrolled call site. It is a behavior-preserving plumbing step exactly like steps 1–2 and should be sim-gated the same way.

**Theme B — Rarity needs a named TIER ladder, not just a cleaner "1 in N".**
Backed by: Rarity, Procedural Narrative, Player-Meta (3 of 7).
Why it matters: §8 step 7 fixes the *math* (empirical CDF, delete the floors/sqrt fudge) but leaves rarity as a bare float that players cannot compare, brag about, or feel. Players parse "Legendary" instantly; nobody parses "1 in 411,000" instantly. A small ordered tier band with designer-set cutoffs (in `model-params.json`, population-calibrated by the sim) gives an instant legible verdict, a collection axis, and a CI-checkable target share per tier. step 7 is the natural home and the moment to define cut points from the real distribution.

**Theme C — A balance *diff* dashboard, not just PASS/FAIL.**
Backed by: Live-Ops, Balance/Telemetry (2 of 7, near-identical proposals).
Why it matters: §7 makes the harness exit non-zero, but a designer can add an event that passes every aggregate invariant while quietly making "war" the modal event in a continent — invisible to a wall of PASS lines. Both personas want `simulate.mjs` to emit a machine-readable `sim-report.json` (per-event fire rate, per-career occupation share, band/class distribution, rarity histogram), commit a baseline, and diff it on every PR. This is the substrate that makes "verify each step against `npm run sim`" (§8 closing line) a real review gate instead of eyeballing stdout.

**Theme D — Promote validation to versioned JSON Schema files, author-time not just load-time.**
Backed by: Live-Ops, Moddability (2 of 7).
Why it matters: §4's `load.js` validates at `makeRoller` time (runtime), but a designer adding an event needs the error in their editor with line numbers, before commit. Both want real `data/schema/*.schema.json` files (replacing the unenforceable `_schema` *comment* in `careers.json`, §3.2) wired into an `npm run validate` CI step alongside `npm run sim`. The schema artifact doubles as the published content/mod contract — the expensive 90% (centralized, fail-loud load) is already in the doc; this is the one inch further that makes the contract a thing you can hand to a stranger.

**Theme E — Datafy the storytelling layer (copy as grammar / chains), not just the rows.**
Backed by: Procedural Narrative, Live-Ops (2 of 7, from different angles).
Why it matters: §3.3 moves text to `copy.json` but preserves the primitive "one event = one frozen clause, picked uniformly." With ~45% of lives carrying an event over ~42 events, repeat spinners see the literal string "battled a serious illness" over and over. Narrative wants a slotted grammar (trigger + context fragment + aftermath fragment, conditioned on data the card already computes — `prec`, `|w|`, severity); Live-Ops wants copy keyed by stable ids with a coverage linter (the existing `disaster || DISASTER.Asia` fallback is a silent content hole). Both agree the schema must be designed for this *before* step 5 and §4 validation freeze it.

**Theme F — The migration freezes greenfield schemas; get the load-bearing fields in NOW.**
Backed by: Procedural Narrative, Moddability, Live-Ops, Determinism (4 of 7, applied to different fields).
Why it matters: §4 `load.js` validation will calcify the `events.json`/`copy.json`/`careers.json` shapes the moment step 5 ships. Each persona names a different field that is one-line-to-add now and a coordinated migration later: `theme`/affinity tags + a `spawns` edge (Narrative), `version`/`availability` for seasonal content (Live-Ops), `namespace:id` + `schemaVersion` (Moddability), version-pin provenance (Determinism). The shared lesson: decide the schema's *future* obligations before it's frozen.

## 4. Tensions & disagreements

**T1 — "No behavior change" vs. "the refactor must improve the game."**
The doc explicitly scopes itself to internal quality (§ intro: "not to change model *behavior*"). Player-Meta and Narrative push hard against this, arguing the team could burn all 9 steps and ship zero player-facing value. **My call:** Keep the migration's no-behavior-change discipline *within each step* — it's what makes the steps reviewable and is non-negotiable for steps 1–6. But the doc's *scope* is wrong to stop at step 9. Rarity tiers (step 7) and the seed primitive (step 0.5) are *in-scope* model concerns the doc already half-touches; the dex, daily seed, and signature cards are explicit UI follow-ons that should be named as a "Phase 2" appendix so they aren't lost. The refactor isn't the place to build the dex, but it *is* the place to stop throwing away `prestige` and to make rarity legible. Reframe the doc's goal from "clean the model" to "clean the model *and* stop dropping the collectible signals it already computes."

**T2 — Fail-loud-at-startup (§4, §7) vs. graceful degradation for untrusted content (Moddability).**
The doc's instinct — any validation failure throws, sim exits non-zero — is correct for first-party data and *wrong* for third-party mod packs, where one bad row bricking everyone's game is the #1 support nightmare. **My call:** Both are right, scoped by trust tier. Core data: strict, fail-loud, harness exits non-zero (keep §4/§7 exactly). Mod packs (if/when they exist): lenient — quarantine the bad pack, boot on remaining valid content, surface a load report. This is a clean addition, not a contradiction; the blast radius should scale with how much you trust the source. But this only matters once mods are a goal — see T4.

**T3 — Is the moddability investment in-scope at all?**
Moddability argues the refactor "accidentally builds 80% of a mod system" and the team should name it as a goal now. Live-Ops wants the same tooling for *internal* content velocity, not mods. **My call:** Adopt the moddability-shaped *decisions* that are cheap now and catastrophic later — versioned schemas (#5), `schemaVersion` + migrator (#29), the fn-registry trust boundary (#16), copy-as-swappable-layer (keep IDs separate from prose). *Defer* the parts that only pay off with an actual mod ecosystem — namespace merging (#31), lenient mod-pack loading (T2). Justification: the schema/version decisions serve internal Live-Ops velocity *today* regardless of whether mods ever ship, so they pay for themselves; the merge/quarantine machinery is speculative until there's a third party to merge. Don't declare "mod system" a goal — declare "data contract" a goal, which both personas actually need.

**T4 — RATE: relocate (doc, step 2) vs. decompose (Balance).**
The doc moves `RATE` into `model-params.json`; Balance argues this relocates the coupling without removing it — every event's effective rate stays a function of every other's. **My call:** Balance is right and the doc even names this exact smell in §6 while the migration fails to fix it. But *decomposing* RATE (sim-calibrated aggregate multiplier over honest per-event bases) is a real behavior-sensitive change, not the "very low risk" relocation step 2 claims. Split it: step 2 relocates RATE as-is (truly zero-risk plumbing); add a *later* step (after the seed primitive and the per-event rate table exist, so it's measurable) that decouples it. Don't smuggle a balance change into a plumbing step.

**T5 — Narrative ambition (`spawns` chains, director, grammar) vs. migration reviewability.**
Narrative's highest-impact asks (#9 spawns is L-effort, the director #11) are genuine new engine behavior, conflicting with the doc's "resist one big rewrite" (§8). **My call:** Honor the schema-design asks now (Theme F) — `theme` tags, a `spawns` *field*, grammar-shaped copy entries cost one schema decision each and are free before step 5 freezes the format. *Build* the engine that consumes them (second resolver pass, director generalization, grammar assembly) as a *post-migration* phase, sim-gated like everything else. The line: design the data shape during the refactor, build the new engine after. This gets Narrative 80% of the future value at 5% of the migration risk.

**T6 — Where does rarity weighting authority live (Rarity's fortune-score) vs. the doc's "empirical CDF"?**
Rarity wants a *designed* weighted fortune scalar; the doc says "empirical fortune CDF" (let the data speak). These are subtly opposed — empirical means the curve shape is discovered, designed means the team picks the axis weights. **My call:** Rarity is right that pure-empirical reproduces the original sin (rarity = whatever the tails multiply to, hence the "96-year-old nurse" legendary). Adopt the hybrid the persona actually proposes: designer-owned *weights* producing the scalar F, then *one* empirically-calibrated CDF mapping F→"1 in N". The team owns *what makes a life rare* (weights, in data); the sim owns *how fast N climbs in the tail* (CDF calibration). That's both "designed" and "empirical" without contradiction, and it's the version that makes the per-tier-share CI assertion (#7) meaningful.

## 5. What the refactor is MISSING for the game

1. **Seeded, reproducible RNG (the foundational gap).** The doc claims "the model is pure given complete validated data" (§4) but never addresses the entropy source — the global RNG everywhere plus `randn`'s mutable `_spare` global. Purity-in-data is not purity-in-RNG. **Slot:** new **step 0.5**, immediately before §8 step 1, gated identically (population stats unchanged within tol). Everything in §5 below depends on it.

2. **Reproducibility/version contract.** No `(modelVersion, dataVersion, seed)` triple, so a shared roll can silently drift when the sim recalibrates `bands.json`. **Slot:** a 4th `load.js` job in step 1 (sibling of `_imputed` provenance), with calibration outputs (§3.1) folded into `dataVersion`.

3. **Collection meta.** No dex, no rarity tiers, no personal-best, no achievement vocabulary — and `prestige` is authored, kept as SSoT, and rendered nowhere. **Slot:** rarity *tiers* land *in* step 7; the dex/personal-best are a named UI Phase-2 that reuses step-7 tiers and step-3 tags. Validate the `prestige` enum in step 3's tag work.

4. **Designer tooling.** No author-time schema, no balance diff dashboard, no runtime-tunable drop rates, no content scaffolder. The catalog is a destination, not a pipeline. **Slot:** schema + `npm run validate` in step 1; `sim-report.json` baseline-diff in step 0 (it strengthens every later gate); runtime-tunable `tuning.json` extends step 2; scaffolder after step 5.

5. **A narrative engine, not just a clean catalog.** Independent memoryless Bernoulli rolls, a 2-event greedy cap, one causal edge, one authored arc. Moving frozen strings into `copy.json` produces the same causality-free cards. **Slot:** design the schema fields (`theme`, `spawns`, grammar-shaped copy, session de-dup memory) *before* step 5 freezes the format; build the consuming engine (2nd resolver pass, director, grammar) as a post-migration phase.

6. **Provenance *consumed*, not just stamped.** §4 stamps `_imputed` but nothing uses it; a single fallback constant applied to 55 countries is a stealth global balance lever. **Slot:** provenance-segmented invariants in the harness work alongside step 1.

7. **RATE decoupling.** §6 names the coupling; the migration never fixes it (step 2 only relocates). **Slot:** a dedicated post-step-2 sub-step once the per-event rate table (step 5 prep) makes it measurable.

## 6. First three moves

1. **Insert step 0.5: thread one injectable seeded PRNG through `makeRoller`, delete `randn`'s `_spare` global, make `roll.js` pass `rand` — gated as zero-behavior-change like steps 1–2.** It is the foundation for the harness's own safety claims and every meta feature; it only gets harder to retrofit each later step.
2. **Upgrade step 0/1: make `simulate.mjs` exit non-zero AND emit `sim-report.json` from a fixed seed with a committed baseline-diff, validating against versioned `data/schema/*.json` — turning the gate from PASS/FAIL text into a reviewable, exact-replay balance tool.**
3. **Before step 5 freezes the schema, design `events.json`/`copy.json` for the future: grammar-shaped copy entries, a `theme` tag, a `spawns` edge, and `version`/`availability` — then validate the existing `prestige` enum in step 3 so the collectible signal stops hitting the floor.**
